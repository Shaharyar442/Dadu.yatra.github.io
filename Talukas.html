Dadu

Dadu city is a town in Sindh, Pakistan. Dadu is the capital of Dadu District. According to the 2017 Census of Pakistan its population was 171,191.[1] The town is located on the Western bank of River Indus and is administratively subdivided into three Union councils. It comes in the district of HUB.






Mehar

Mehar Taluka Sindhi is an administrative subdivision taluka of Dadu District in the Sindh province of Pakistan.


According to records, in 1848 Mehar Taluka was created as a subdivision of Dadu District during British rule with the town of Mehar as the headquarters. The taluka, along with the rest of Sindh, was for a time part of the Bombay Presidency of British India.

Johi

Johi is a small town of Dadu District, Sindh province, Pakistan which is located between Balochistan and the Sindh province border.[1] Johi town[2] is also Taluka of Dadu District.[3] A civil hospital is also in Johi.[4] Johi is commonly known as the historical area and has many villages like Haji Khan Hairo Khan, Bazmaal Khoso, Drigh Bala, Rajaghanda and many similar villages

It has many visiting places people here depend on the rain water and do crops as land is very rich in minerals. It has historical town Wahi Pandhi and at a distance of 50 km from Wahi Pandhi Taluka Johi there is Gorakh Hill Station. Which is situated in Kirthar Mountains Range and in mountainous area of Johi many water streams and hill torrents known as Nai Gaj, Taqi, Nali
